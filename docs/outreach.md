# Veritas — outreach pack

Ready-to-send messages, a 15-min call agenda, a response tracker, and an evidence checklist. The goal everywhere is one named adoption signal: **"yes, we'd pilot this" / a usable quote / a willingness-to-pay signal.**

Honesty rule: this is a hackathon prototype seeking **design partners**, not a finished product. Say so. Don't oversell.

Hero numbers (measured live, reproducible seeded run): federated detection **0.955 vs 0.637** siloed → **~£296k fraud prevented**, **1,160 fewer victims**, detection **hours not days**, **0 customer records transmitted**.

## 2. The 15-minute call agenda

**Mins 0–2 — Frame (honest).** One prototype, one weekend, FLock × UK Sovereign AI hackathon. We're seeking design partners, not selling. Their time buys us a reality check.

**Mins 2–8 — Show.** Live two-regime demo: inject a cross-bank mule campaign → siloed stays red, federated greens out in hours. `customerRecordsTransmitted: 0` badge. Inject a malicious member → Multi-Krum rejects the poisoned update; provenance log records it. Land the numbers: **0.955 vs 0.637 → ~£296k prevented, 1,160 fewer victims**.

**Mins 8–14 — Ask (the three questions that win the rubric):**
1. *Latency today:* "When a new mule typology appears, how long before it's shared across banks/members today — hours, days, weeks?" (We expect "days". Get them to say it.)
2. *Willingness to pay:* "If a federated model cut that to hours with zero data leaving you, is that something you'd pilot — and is it a line item you'd budget for, given PSR liability?"
3. *Quote:* "Could we attribute a one-line view from you on the slide — even just 'this addresses a real gap'?"

**Min 14–15 — Close.** Confirm the single most useful next step (intro to a member, a follow-up with a technical lead, or permission to quote). Send a one-line thank-you the same day capturing what they said.

## 4. Evidence to collect (tied to the rubric)

The rubric lists **"credible path to real-world adoption."** From every conversation, capture four things and put them on the slide: (1) the **current cross-bank sharing latency in their own words** — ideally "days" or "weeks" — which is the wedge the whole pitch turns on; (2) a verbatim **pain quote** about the cost or slowness of today's siloed detection; (3) any **willingness-to-pay signal** — "we'd pilot this," "that's budgeted under PSR liability," even "we'd want to see X first"; and (4) **one named supportive voice** (person + org) we can attribute, however hedged. One real "this addresses a gap we feel" from a named fraud lead beats any number we simulated.
