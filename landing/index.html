01 — The problem

Authorised Push Payment fraud is the UK's largest fraud category. It is networked by design.

Mule networks span banks on purpose — so no single institution ever sees the whole pattern.

Criminals move money across many accounts at many banks before any one of them can connect the dots. Each bank holds a fragment; none holds the picture. And banks legally cannot pool customer data to assemble it.

With VeritasOnce one bank confirmed a receiving account as a mule, that behaviour could have improved every member's model — similar accounts reviewed or frozen before further withdrawals.

With VeritasBanks could have collectively learned the receiving-account pattern — funds from many unrelated victims, high velocity. A pattern confirmed at one bank strengthens detection at the others.

A pattern confirmed at one bank should make every bank smarter.

02 — Why now

Regulation just turned cross-bank fraud into a board-level liability.

The economics changed in October 2024.

PSR mandate

The receiving bank is now liable

Since October 2024 the Payment Systems Regulator splits APP reimbursement 50/50 between the sending and receiving institution.

The consequence

Fraud arriving from elsewhere is now your cost

Detecting mules that land from another bank moved from "nice to have" to a direct, attributable line on the P&L.

The blocker

You still can't share the data

GDPR, competition law and commercial sensitivity make pooling customer transactions a non-starter. The need is new; the constraint is not.

03 — The approach

A sovereign intelligence network — collective learning, no central data lake.

Each bank runs a sealed node behind its own firewall. It trains locally on its own books and shares only privacy-protected model updates. Veritas aggregates them into one network model — built with differential privacy, an ensemble of ML methods, and FLock for federated-learning orchestration.

AThe node trains locally — raw records never leave the bank.
BUpdates are clipped, differentially-private, and Byzantine-checked by Multi-Krum before they count.
CEvery contribution and rejection is written to a signed, tamper-evident log.
